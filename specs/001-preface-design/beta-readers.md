# Beta Reader Recruitment Plan

**Created**: 2025-11-10  
**Purpose**: Identify and recruit 4 beta readers (1 per persona) for preface validation  
**Feature**: 001-preface-design

---

## Required Personas (1 Reader Each)

### Persona 1: Student/Beginner (No Coding Experience)
**Profile Needed**:
- NO prior programming experience
- Interested in learning software development
- Concerned about: "Am I too late to learn?" "Is this too hard?"
- Age range: 18-35 (typical student demographic)

**Validation Focus**:
- Is preface welcoming (not intimidating)?
- Can they understand "Specs Are the New Syntax"?
- Do they feel encouraged by "Best Time to Learn" section?
- Do they recognize themselves in "Who This Is For"?

**Recruitment Sources**:
- Online learning communities (Reddit r/learnprogramming, Discord servers)
- Local community colleges or bootcamps
- Friends/family interested in tech career transition

---

### Persona 2: Experienced Developer (Worried About AI Replacing Them)
**Profile Needed**:
- 5+ years professional development experience
- Currently working with traditional development workflows
- Concerned about: "Will AI replace me?" "Is my expertise obsolete?"
- May be skeptical of AI hype

**Validation Focus**:
- Does "Why AI Makes Developers MORE Valuable" address anxiety?
- Is the economic argument credible?
- Can they distinguish Assisted/Driven/Native approaches?
- Do they see professional value in "Specs Are Syntax" message?

**Recruitment Sources**:
- Developer communities (Dev.to, Hacker News, local meetups)
- LinkedIn connections
- Current/former colleagues

---

### Persona 3: Educator (Teaching Programming in AI Era)
**Profile Needed**:
- Teaching programming/CS (university, bootcamp, or online)
- Interested in: How to integrate AI into curriculum
- Concerned about: Pedagogical soundness, student outcomes

**Validation Focus**:
- Does preface demonstrate pedagogical rigor?
- Is the co-learning philosophy sound?
- Would they recommend this book to students?
- Does "Best Time to Learn" resonate with teaching philosophy?

**Recruitment Sources**:
- Academic networks (university CS departments)
- Bootcamp/online course instructors
- EdTech communities

---

### Persona 4: Entrepreneur/Founder (Building AI-Powered Products)
**Profile Needed**:
- Building or considering building AI-powered product
- Technical or non-technical founder
- Interested in: Competitive advantage, speed to market, ROI

**Validation Focus**:
- Does preface communicate practical ROI?
- Is "Specs Are Syntax" compelling for product development?
- Do they see value in AI-Driven Development approach?
- Is scope clear (what book will teach)?

**Recruitment Sources**:
- Startup communities (Y Combinator forums, Indie Hackers)
- LinkedIn founder networks
- Local startup incubators

## Beta Reader Compensation

**Options** (choose based on budget/project):
1. **Free copy of book** (when published)
2. **Acknowledgment** in book credits
3. **Small honorarium** ($50-100 per reader)
4. **Early access** to course materials or community

**Recommendation**: Offer acknowledgment + free copy minimum

## Feedback Collection Method

**For Each Phase**:
1. Send preface section(s) as Markdown or PDF
2. Provide specific questions based on validation focus
3. Request response within 3-5 days
4. Optional: Schedule 15-minute video call for deeper feedback
