# Chapter 1: The Agent Factory Paradigm

Welcome to the foundational chapter that establishes the mental models you need for AI-native development. By the end of these nine lessons, you'll understand not just *that* AI is changing software development, but *how* to think about this transformation strategically.

## What You'll Learn

This chapter builds your conceptual framework through three progressions:

**Evidence & Scale (Lessons 1-2)**
- Concrete data proving 2024-2025 is a genuine inflection point
- The economic scale of the transformation ($3 trillion developer economy)
- Why this disruption is fundamentally different from previous technology shifts

**The Two Paths Framework (Lessons 3-6)**
- How to distinguish **General Agents** (Claude Code, Gemini CLI) from **Custom Agents** (OpenAI SDK, Claude SDK)
- The key insight: General Agents *build* Custom Agents
- Your evolving role from coder to orchestrator
- The four generations of AI development tools

- The three-layer AI development stack
- How Spec-Driven Development enables AI collaboration

## Prerequisites

No prior AI development experience required. This chapter is designed for:
- Developers curious about AI-assisted workflows
- Domain experts exploring AI product opportunities
- Technical leaders assessing AI adoption strategies

## How This Chapter Connects

The concepts in this chapter provide the vocabulary and mental models used throughout the book:
- **Part 2** builds on the Spec-Driven Development preview in Lesson 9
- **Part 3** implements the Three Roles framework introduced here
- **Parts 6-9** teach the Custom Agent SDKs mentioned in Lesson 3

## Key Concepts You'll Master

By completing this chapter, you'll be able to:

1. **Cite evidence** for the 2024-2025 AI inflection point
2. **Distinguish** General Agents from Custom Agents
3. **Explain** the orchestrator role shift
4. **Identify** which generation a tool belongs to
5. **Name** the Five Powers that enable autonomous agents
6. **Describe** the three-layer AI stack architecture
7. **Outline** the Spec-Driven Development workflow
